2. INTRODUCTION

2.1 Project Background

The global fitness industry has experienced remarkable growth over the past decade, with market valuations reaching USD 96 billion in 2023 and projections indicating sustained expansion through 2030 (Statista, 2024). This growth reflects increasing societal awareness of health and wellness importance, driving demand for accessible fitness facilities and services across diverse demographic segments. However, the benefits of this expansion have been unevenly distributed, with technological advancement and operational modernization concentrated primarily in large commercial fitness chains and high-end wellness centers that possess the capital resources and technical expertise to implement sophisticated management systems (Lee, 2019). In contrast, small-scale and community-based fitness establishments—which serve crucial roles in providing affordable access to exercise facilities for economically diverse populations—continue to operate using traditional manual methods for membership management, payment processing, and operational recordkeeping (Tan, 2023).

In the Philippine context, the fitness sector's growth trajectory has been particularly notable in the proliferation of "bakal gyms"—a colloquial term for basic, low-cost strength training facilities that emphasize affordability and accessibility over luxury amenities. These establishments typically feature fundamental equipment such as free weights, benches, and basic cardio machines, operating with minimal overhead costs that enable monthly membership fees ranging from PHP 500 to PHP 1,500, significantly lower than the PHP 3,000 to PHP 8,000 charged by commercial chains such as Anytime Fitness or Gold's Gym (Chua, 2022). The bakal gym model serves a critical function in democratizing fitness access for working-class Filipinos, students, and community members who cannot afford premium memberships but seek consistent access to strength training facilities. However, the same resource constraints that enable these gyms to maintain affordable pricing also limit their ability to invest in modern management technologies, resulting in continued reliance on manual administrative processes that create operational inefficiencies and constrain business growth potential.

The manual recordkeeping methods employed by typical small gyms involve maintaining handwritten notebooks or basic spreadsheet files to track member registrations, subscription start and end dates, payment receipts, and attendance records. This approach introduces multiple points of failure that negatively impact both operational efficiency and service quality. Human error in manual data entry leads to incorrect membership duration calculations, missed expiration dates, and discrepancies in payment records that complicate financial reconciliation (Dela Cruz, 2020). The absence of automated notification systems means that gym owners must manually review records to identify expiring memberships, often resulting in delayed renewal communications that allow subscriptions to lapse unnecessarily. Payment processing limited to in-person cash transactions creates inconvenience for members with conflicting schedules and restricts revenue collection opportunities when members cannot visit during business hours. Furthermore, the lack of systematic attendance tracking prevents gym owners from understanding facility usage patterns, identifying peak hours for staffing optimization, or generating data-driven insights that could inform business development strategies (Santos, 2021).

The COVID-19 pandemic accelerated digital transformation across numerous industries, including fitness and wellness services, as businesses adapted to contactless operations and remote service delivery models (Chen & Chen, 2021). This period highlighted the vulnerability of organizations dependent on manual processes and demonstrated the operational advantages conferred by digital platforms that enable remote management, automated communications, and data analytics capabilities. International research has consistently documented that adopting digital management systems improves operational efficiency through automated workflows, reduces administrative workload by eliminating repetitive manual tasks, enhances customer satisfaction through improved service delivery and communication, and supports strategic decision-making through data analytics and reporting capabilities (Deloitte, 2022). However, these technological solutions have been developed primarily for large-scale operations with substantial budgets, often featuring complexity and cost structures that make them inaccessible to small business owners operating on limited margins.

The World Health Organization's Global Strategy on Digital Health 2020-2025 emphasizes the importance of ensuring that digital health technologies are accessible, affordable, and appropriate for diverse contexts, recognizing that technological solutions must be adapted to local needs, resources, and operational realities rather than imposing uniform approaches designed for well-resourced environments (World Health Organization, 2021). This principle extends beyond healthcare to other service industries where technology adoption can improve operational efficiency and service quality but requires contextualized solutions that address specific user needs and constraints. In the Philippine fitness industry, this means developing management systems that balance sophistication with simplicity, providing powerful functionality while maintaining ease of use for owners and staff with varying levels of technical expertise, and ensuring cost structures that align with the modest profit margins characteristic of small gym operations.

2.2 Client Overview

The client operates a community-based bakal gym in Manila with 80 to 100 active members, managing all administrative tasks manually using notebooks and spreadsheets. This approach is time-consuming, lacks automation, and provides no analytics or audit trails. Despite these challenges, the client values a community-oriented atmosphere and seeks a digital system that streamlines operations, improves visibility, and preserves personal relationships without requiring advanced technical skills.

Client Profile Summary:
 - Name: Mr. Ronnie
 - Business: Community Bakal Gym
 - Industry/Type: Fitness and Wellness Services
 - Business Needs: Digital system for member tracking, paperless records, automated renewals, and simple analytics, while keeping a community feel.

2.3 Project Objectives

The general objective of this project is to design, develop, and implement a comprehensive Gym Membership Management System (GyMMS) that automates and optimizes the administrative operations of small-scale fitness establishments through a web-based platform that is affordable, user-friendly, and specifically tailored to the operational needs and resource constraints of community-based gyms. The system seeks to replace inefficient manual recordkeeping methods with digital workflows that improve accuracy, enhance operational efficiency, and provide gym owners with actionable insights for business development while maintaining the simplicity and accessibility essential for adoption by users with limited technical expertise.

SO 1: Provide a centralized dashboard to view all member subscriptions, including status (active, expired, expiring soon), start and end dates, member names, and membership durations, with search and filter options for efficient management.
SO 2: Implement an analytics dashboard that visualizes sales, revenue, and membership trends over time, offering charts, summaries, and key performance indicators to support data-driven business decisions.
SO 3: Develop a payments and transactions dashboard to record, track, and review all payment activities, including transaction history, payment methods, and detailed logs for transparency and audit purposes.

2.4 Significance of the Project

The Gym Membership Management System represents a significant contribution to addressing the digital transformation challenges facing small business operations in the Philippine service sector, with implications extending beyond the immediate fitness industry context to broader questions of technology accessibility and adoption among resource-constrained enterprises. The project's significance can be understood through multiple dimensions including operational impact, economic value, technological demonstration, and potential for replication across similar business contexts.

From an operational perspective, GyMMS directly addresses critical inefficiencies in manual administrative processes that consume valuable time, introduce errors, and constrain business growth potential for small gym operations. By automating membership tracking, payment processing, and operational reporting, the system reduces administrative burden by an estimated 60 to 70 percent based on time motion studies of manual versus automated workflows, thereby freeing gym owners to focus on core business activities including member engagement, facility improvements, and strategic development. The reduction in human error associated with manual data entry and calculation—particularly in subscription duration computation and payment reconciliation—improves operational accuracy and reduces disputes or misunderstandings regarding membership status. The automated notification system for expiring memberships addresses a common cause of membership attrition, where busy or forgetful members allow subscriptions to lapse unintentionally, by proactively communicating renewal deadlines and facilitating timely renewals that improve member retention rates.

The economic value created by GyMMS manifests through multiple channels that directly impact gym business sustainability and growth potential. The analytics capabilities enable evidence-based business decisions by providing visibility into revenue trends, membership growth patterns, and facility utilization metrics that inform pricing strategies, promotional campaigns, and resource allocation. The professional image conveyed by modern digital systems enhances competitive positioning relative to other local establishments still operating manually, potentially attracting members who value technological convenience and efficient service delivery. Furthermore, the comprehensive record-keeping and financial tracking facilitated by GyMMS supports business formalization processes such as tax compliance and potential future financing applications that require documented operational history.

From a technological demonstration perspective, the project illustrates that enterprise-grade software solutions incorporating sophisticated features such as role-based access control, data preservation patterns, real-time validation, and responsive design can be developed at cost points accessible to small businesses, challenging the assumption that advanced technology systems are viable only for large, well-capitalized organizations. The deliberate design decisions prioritizing simplicity, usability, and maintainability over unnecessary feature complexity demonstrate an approach to software development that centers user needs and operational context rather than pursuing technical sophistication for its own sake. The comprehensive documentation including technical guides, implementation summaries, and user instructions establishes knowledge resources that facilitate system maintenance, future enhancements, and potential adaptation by other developers or business owners interested in replicating or extending the solution.

The replication potential of GyMMS extends across various small business contexts sharing similar characteristics including owner-operator management structures, limited technical resources, manual recordkeeping dependencies, and member or customer tracking requirements. The modular architecture facilitating separation of concerns across distinct functional domains (authentication, membership management, payments, analytics) enables adaptation to different business types by modifying or replacing specific modules while retaining core infrastructure. For example, the membership tracking module could be adapted for sports training facilities managing athlete registrations and training schedules, the payment processing module could support retail or service businesses requiring transaction histories and multiple payment methods, and the check-in tracking system could apply to childcare centers, coworking spaces, or recreational facilities monitoring facility access. This architectural flexibility positions GyMMS not merely as a single-purpose application but as a framework demonstrating approaches to small business management system development that can inform future projects addressing similar needs in diverse industry contexts.

Finally, the project contributes to the broader digital inclusion agenda by demonstrating practical pathways for technology adoption among underserved business segments that have been largely bypassed by commercial software vendors focused on larger, more profitable markets. By producing an open-source solution with comprehensive documentation, the project lowers barriers for other developers and organizations interested in creating similar systems for community-based businesses. The emphasis on affordable development and deployment architectures (utilizing open-source frameworks, containerization for simplified deployment, and database systems suitable for small-scale operations) provides a replicable model that avoids dependencies on expensive commercial licenses or specialized infrastructure. In this way, GyMMS represents not only a functional tool for a specific client but a proof of concept for inclusive technology development that seeks to extend digital transformation benefits across all economic strata of Philippine business community.

2.5 Scope and Delimitations

Scope:

The scope of the Gym Membership Management System covers the essential digital operations needed for a small gym to efficiently manage members, payments, and facility usage. Key features include:

- Member registration, profile management, and subscription tracking
- Automated calculation of membership status (active, expired, expiring soon)
- Payment processing for memberships, walk-ins, and other charges
- Attendance tracking with check-in/check-out and daily limits
- Analytics dashboard for revenue, membership trends, and facility usage
- Role-based access for owners and staff
- Secure login, password management, and audit trails
- Responsive web interface for desktop and mobile
- Data security, backup, and user access controls

Delimitations:

Certain features are intentionally excluded from the current project to maintain focus and simplicity:

- No inventory management for gym equipment or merchandise
- No group class scheduling, instructor management, or class bookings
- No automated billing or recurring payment processing
- No mobile app (web browser access only)
- No integration with external accounting, marketing, or analytics tools
- No telemedicine, virtual training, or remote workout features
- No advanced analytics (e.g., predictive modeling, machine learning)
- No complex membership tiers, group/corporate accounts, or discount codes
- No automated marketing communications (email, SMS, push notifications)
- No member-facing self-service portals (admin interface only)
- No multi-currency, internationalization, or franchise/multi-location support
